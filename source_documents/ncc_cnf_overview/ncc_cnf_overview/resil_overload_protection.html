Overload protection

If traffic exceeds what the application can handle, NCC has built-in overload protection where requests are shed to avoid overwhelming the system. Administrators can configure overload protection thresholds in the Service Manager application for both charging and servicemanager. All monitoring is done at the pod level and the overload protections are applied only to the individual pods that are in trouble.

Overload protection uses a multi-level escalation model. The system takes into account whether a pod is above a high threshold and for how long they have remained above the high threshold. Increasingly aggressive remedies are configured as overload states. Remedies are attempted and the system waits to see their effect before escalating to a higher overload state with a more aggressive remedy. An administrator can configure a conservative remedy like rejecting ten percent of traffic from certain non-critical APIs. A typical configuration starts to shed new sessions first, while allowing existing sessions to continue to be processed as the priority. If a configurable escalation interval expires and the pod remains over the threshold, the overload state is increased and a new more aggressive remedy is attempted. This sequence of escalating and measuring the effect continues until it reaches the top resource critical level.

Issues found with the pod are configurable as a low threshold, high threshold and critical resource threshold. While a high threshold triggers a sequence of remedy attempts with time to verify their effect, a pod that exceeds the resource critical threshold immediately jumps to the highest overload state with the most aggressive remedy. This avoids reacting too slowly when a pod is in serious trouble. As the pod recovers, it must fall below the low threshold to de-escalate to the next level down (and eventually back to normal state). In case the measured threshold is below the high but above the low, the pod remains in its current overload state with the current remedy still in effect.

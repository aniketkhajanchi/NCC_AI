Late rating streams

In terms of NCC CDR streams, late rating refers to the receiving and processing of input files in a specific ASCII format, which contain records of roaming Voice MO calls, and converting the files into Diameter (IMS Ro) requests to be sent to the Charging application. This type of processing is done by specific late rating streams as follows:
  • Late rating input stream is a scheduled stream that reads files from an SFTP server, parses them into records, validates them, and then stores them in the database. See Late rating input stream for more information.
  • Late rating business logic stream maps the late rating records into charging compliant records by extracting the charging information from the parsed records fields. See Late rating business logic stream for more information.
  • Late rating Diameter distributor stream sends the late rating records to the Charging application as Diameter requests. See Late rating Diameter distributor stream for more information.
  • Late rating log distributor stream takes both the Diameter requests that Late rating Diameter distributor stream fails to distribute and the input records that the Late Rating Input fails to parse and stores them to a remote server. See Late rating log distributor stream for more information.

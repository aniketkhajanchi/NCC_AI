Carrying forward delta volume from before BCR/CS to after BCR/CS

In Gy calls if usage granularity is used, after rounding the usage delta volume is calculated as the difference between rounding volume and the actual volume coming from the network to consume. This delta volume is re-used in the next CCR of the call.

By default, if BCR/TTC occurs during a Gy call, delta volume calculated before BCR/TTC is not carry forwarded to the usage after BCR or CS change.

To carry forward delta volume calculated before BCR/CS to the usage after BCR or CS change, set the application preference Rounding Units At TTC to ENABLED_FOR_GY_N40. Refer to the Global Configuration Guide for details.

In case of bundle expiry at BCR/TTC, delta volume is not carried forward. Usage after TTC is discarded. The discarded units (after adjusting lost volume) is presented in the lostVolume tag in the CDR.

Example:

Consider a granularity of 1 MB.

  1. USU reported in CCR-U is 5.2 MB.
    1. After applying the granularity factor, 6 MB is committed.
    2. Delta volume = 6 – 5.2 = 0.8 MB.
  2. In the next CCR-U, USU reported is 5.3 MB.
    1. After adjusting the delta volume of previous CCR-U, 5.3 – 0.8 MB = 4.5 MB is considered for charging.
    2. After applying the granularity factor, 5 MB is committed.
    3. Delta volume = 5 – 4.5 = 0.5 MB.
  3. In CCR-T, USU reported is 6.1 MB.
    1. After adjusting the delta volume of previous CCR-U, 6.1 – 0.5 MB = 5.6 MB is considered for charging.
    2. After applying the granularity factor, 6 MB is committed.
    3. Delta volume = 6 – 5.6 = 0.4 MB.

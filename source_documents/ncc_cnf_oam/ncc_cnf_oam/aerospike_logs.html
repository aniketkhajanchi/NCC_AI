Aerospike logs

By default, Aerospike logs are kept on a persistent volume attached to the pod. The pod handles log rotation internally, and logs with warning or higher severity level are directed to stdout for collection by the Kubernetes logging management subsystem. This system of logging provides a few advantages:

  • The logs can be shared with Aerospike support in their original format.
  • The logs are accessible after a restart.
  • Aerospike logs are very verbose. Many histogram and runtime information are logged at 10 seconds intervals. Keeping these logs in the persistent volume can reduce the stress on the Kubernetes logging management system.

While this is the default Aerospike logging behavior, the setting aerospike.logClaim.enabled may be set to false to disable this feature.

For information regarding log level configuration on Aerospike, refer to the Aerospike documentation found at this link: How to change log levels in Aerospike.

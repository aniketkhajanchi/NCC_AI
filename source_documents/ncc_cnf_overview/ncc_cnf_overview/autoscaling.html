Autoscaling

NCC uses autoscaling on some of its pods to enable growth during periods of increased demand. This accounts for the typical significant ebbs and flows during busy hour verses overnight that providers experience. It also allows the system to deal with unexpected surges or decreases in traffic.

Kubernetes monitors the pods and automatically adds more when the average CPU usage exceeds the configured thresholds, or removes pods when CPU usage decreases.

In environments where worker nodes can be dynamically added such as public clouds, increases in pods trigger an increase in worker nodes.

Combining both pod scaling and worker node scaling can enable a high degree of flexibility and cost savings.

Assume Positive streams

Assume Positive (AP) is a feature required for handling situations where the CHF goes down or is unavailable. Assume Positive is typically implemented in the network elements to facilitate the allocation of temporary quota for a data or call session in case of temporary errors in CHF. If the CHF overall infrastructure does not respond or returns an error, PGW, SMF, or IMS can allocate temporary traffic allowance to avoid traffic blocking. If a session with the CHF cannot be restored later, network elements generate Assume Positive logs, which the CHF collects periodically reprocesses out of session.

Figure: Assume Positive architecture

In NCC CGF, Assume Positive functionality is handled by two main streams - Assume Positive collector stream and Assume Positive handler stream. Additionally, the Assume Positive input stream picks up records that Assume Positive handler stream is not able to process. Finally, the Business logic stream can map the Assume Positive disposition logs as generic records. For more information, see

  • Assume Positive handler stream
  • Kafka disposition log collector stream

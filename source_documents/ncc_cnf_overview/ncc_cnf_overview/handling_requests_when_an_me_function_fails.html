Handling requests when an ME subsystem fails

When an ME subsystem fails and is recognized as being out of service, its paired mate subsystem takes over ownership of the device records.

Network traffic arrives at any in service NCC ME subsystem and is routed to the secondary NCC ME subsystem for the subscriber. The secondary NCC ME subsystem processes the traffic normally, perform lifecycle updates (bill cycle resets, etc.), and sends RARs and ASRs for its secondary subscribers.

API traffic from any IG subsystem is sent to the secondary NCC ME subsystem for the subscriber.

In case the ME fails but has not yet been recognized as out of service by the secondary ME, the secondary ME rejects requests with a Diameter UNABLE_TO_DELIVER/UNABLE_TO_COMPLY or HTTP 503 message. Transactions that are sent to the failed NCC by a Diameter peer or 5G client timeout or receive a similar error message.

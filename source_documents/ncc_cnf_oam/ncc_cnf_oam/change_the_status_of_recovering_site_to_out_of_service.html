Set recovering system to out of service

Run these commands on the recovering system to change the status to Out Of Service (OOS).

kubectl -it exec <admincli> -c <container> -n <namespace> -- /opt/tpa/bin/configure-manual-oos -d ME

# Verify MOOS status should change to "TRUE", for IG also verify for SM
kubectl -it exec <admincli> -c <container> -n <namespace> -- /opt/tpa/bin/configure-manual-oos -s ME
Where:
  • <admincli> is the name of the admincli pod.
  • <container> is the name of the container.
  • <namespace> is the name of the namespace.

This example shows the expected output with growthme-admincli-b6496b469-hlmwb as <admincli>, growthme-admincli as <container>, and sps-btel-me5 as <namespace>.

[root@bcmtvzrm3-control-01 me5]# kubectl -it exec growthme-admincli-b6496b469-hlmwb -c growthme-admincli -n sps-btel-me5 -- /opt/tpa/bin/configure-manual-oos -d ME
[root@bcmtvzrm3-control-01 me5]# kubectl -it exec growthme-admincli-b6496b469-hlmwb -c growthme-admincli -n sps-btel-me5 -- /opt/tpa/bin/configure-manual-oos -s ME

NCC subsystem level resiliency

NCC groups services into two logical subsystems: the SM subsystem for GUI and API requests, and the ME subsystem for 4G/5G traffic requests and application business logic. The subsystem groupings serve two purposes.

An NCC subsystem is deployed with multiple instances for redundancy. Each subsystem instance is responsible for some of the application data. During a subsystem failure, another subsystem can take over managing its data. Heartbeats every two seconds between the different NCC subsystem instances establish the status of the different subsystems and trigger the failover. This means there should only be a brief interruption in service when an NCC subsystem becomes unavailable.

The NCC subsystem also defines which services are required to deliver its functionality. An NCC service that is vital going DOWN causes the NCC subsystem to be out of service and stop accepting requests. Within a physical deployment, there can be both an SM subsystem and an ME subsystem. One NCC subsystem can be out of service while the other continues to service requests.

Network deployment

The entire solution that is deployed across multiple sites is called the network deployment.

A typical NCC network deployment has one pair of SM subsystems and one or more pairs of ME subsystems. Physically, this is deployed as a pair of IG systems, which include both the SM and the ME subsystems. Additional pairs of ME subsystems may be deployed for purposes of regionalization or additional capacity as standalone ME systems (not part of an IG).

SM subsystem deployment

An NCC network deployment always includes a pair of IG systems, which each include an SM subsystem. The IG systems are typically deployed in geographically diverse locations to maximize resiliency. SM subsystems are active / active, with data replication between them.
Note:
For back-to-back transactions for a given device, group, or account, it is recommended to send those all to the same SM subsystem in order to avoid race conditions.

ME subsystem deployment

An NCC network deployment always includes a pair of IG systems, which each include an ME subsystem. The IG systems are typically deployed in geographically diverse locations to maximize resiliency. ME subsystems are active / active, with data replication between them. A subset of subscribers or devices is primary on one ME subsystem and secondary on the other in the pair, and the remaining subset is secondary on one ME subsystem and primary on the other.

Additional pairs of ME subsystems can be deployed in standalone ME systems as required for additional network capacity.

Additional ME subsystem deployment options

The ME subsystem pairs can be deployed with two routing options:

  • Independent ME subsystem pairs

    In this model, each ME subsystem pair is independent from the other pairs. Received traffic is either processed locally or site-routed to the other ME subsystem in the pair (or is rejected). There is no NCC site routing to an ME subsystem outside of this pair. The operator's network must perform routing to ensure that a particular message arrives to the correct ME subsystem pair, if that is required.

  • Networked ME subsystem pairs

    In this model, all ME subsystems can communicate with each other and know which subscribers are homed on any of the ME subsystems in the network. When a message arrives at an ME subsystem, if the subscriber is not homed on this local ME subsystem, then this ME subsystem can site-route this message to the home ME subsystem anywhere in the network, not limited to just the other ME subsystem in this pair.

NCC overview

NCC is a cloud-native software solution based on Kubernetes principles and offers significant benefits for service providers including software support for 5G, 4G along with SS7 support for legacy voice, SMS, IVR, and USSD traffic.

Characteristics of cloud-native NCC

These are the key feature highlights of NCC.

  • Cloud-native application
    • Infrastructure built around Kubernetes principles

      NCC uses Docker, containers, Helm charts, and more.

    • Packaged as lightweight containers

      Cloud-native applications are a collection of independent and autonomous services, which are packaged as lightweight containers. Unlike virtual machines, containers scale out and scale in rapidly. Since the unit of scaling is a lightweight container, infrastructure utilization is optimized.

    • Dynamic horizontal scaling

      The number of container instances grows and shrinks as needed for handling the varying load. This enables NCC to consume a much lower footprint during typical operating conditions, where the offered traffic is less than the maximum peak dimensioned and when the mate NCC system is healthy and processing its own share of traffic. A load balancer runs L7 protocols that allows NCC to distribute traffic across the container instances on a request-by-request basis.

    • Internal protocols are service mesh friendly

      Service meshes are powerful tools that add power and flexibility to cloud-native applications. NCC uses Istio that helps provide load balancing, transparent mutual TLS authentication, distributed API call tracing, and more.

    • Designed as loosely coupled microservices

      Microservice design allows for breaking up an application into a series of smaller, more specialized parts, each of which communicates with one another across common interfaces such as APIs and REST interfaces like HTTP. A microservice might be provided by a collection of pods working together to provide a service. Services that belong to the same application discover each other through the application runtime, and they exist independent of other services. Microservice design allows for an elastic infrastructure that scales out with efficiency and high performance.

    • Consistent with the evolution towards agile DevOps processes

      Each service of a cloud-native application goes through an independent life cycle, which is managed through an agile DevOps process. Multiple continuous integration and delivery (CI/CD) pipelines work in tandem to deploy and manage a cloud-native application.

  • 5G readiness

    NCC is a low-latency application built for 4G and 5G.

  • Legacy SS7 support

    NCC support legacy voice, SMS, IVR, and USSD over SS7.

  • Agility and efficiency

    The flexibility provided by the Nokia Agile Rule Technology (ART) delivers performance efficiency for highly complex real-time use cases.

  • Use of best-in-class industry standard tools

    NCC supports the best-in-class industry standard tools such as Jaeger, Prometheus, Kafka, OpenSearch, Kiali, Istio, a metrics viewer, and more.

  • Flexible deployment options

    NCC supports deployment into a variety of customer environments. NCC follows standard best practices and is therefore generic and flexible by nature and suitable for integrating into many varied deployment environments. In some cases, the customer environment (whether their own or in a public cloud) may provide all the Kubernetes, load balancers, Istio, telemetry, and other infrastructure. In other cases, the customer may provide a more basic Kubernetes environment and rely on NCC to provide the service mesh and telemetry.

  • Subscriber model

    The NCC subscriber model provides flexibility in configuring and managing all of the data associated with end-users and their devices, subscriptions, usage, and accounts.

  • Focus on groups

    NCC incorporates a group theme, such as family, large enterprise groups, or ad-hoc groups based on rule-based quota allocation algorithms on the Gy interface. The groups are formed for a short period of time to either share a new subscription or share remaining resources on the same account.

  • In-service updates

    NCC supports in-service software updates leveraging the standard Kubernetes rolling update mechanism. NCC continues servicing traffic while pods are updated one-at-a-time and brought back into service.

CDRs for TMF-compliant eCommerce APIs

Overview

TMF-compliant eCommerce APIs generate the following CDRs:

  • Charging the usage of an e-commerce service (charge request)
  • Refunding a charged e-commerce usage before validity expiration (historicalrefund request)
  • Refunding money for e-commerce usages (refund request)
  • Reserving the usage of an e-commerce service (reserve request)
  • Committing a reserved e-commerce usage before validity expiration (commit request)
  • Canceling a reserved e-commerce usage before validity expiration (cancel request)
  • Refunding, automatically, a reserved e-commerce usage at validity expiration

A number of custom tags may be also added via the charging RSV action Add-CDR.

In case shadow balance is available, the used shadow balance and the remaining shadow balance after eCommerce operation are reported respectively in shadowBalanceCommitted and shadowBalanceAfter tags within RecordProperty listOfMscc/mscc/deviceInfo/subscriptionInfo/chargingServiceInfo/accountInfo.

Note:
The used shadow balance is reported with leading sign (- for debiting, + for refunding).

In case of over-commit scenario where the available balance (including reserved balance) is not enough to afford the amount to commit, the lostMoney tag within RecordProperty listOfMscc/mscc is filled in with the missing amount quota.

In case of refund the refunded amount is reported in tag accountBalanceCommitted within RecordProperty listOfMscc/mscc/deviceInfo/subscriptionInfo/chargingServiceInfo/accountInfo with + leading sign

In case of unsuccessful execution, the RecordProperty listOfMscc/mscc/deviceInfo and all its subRecordProperties are omitted from the generated CDR.

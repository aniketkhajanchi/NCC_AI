NCC resiliency

This section explores the different ways that NCC has resiliency engineered into the architecture. As components fail, the goal is for the system to handle the issue and make sure that external requests can continue to be handled and data integrity is maintained.

The resiliency is divided into three levels.
  • At the pod level, redundancy mechanisms ensure that pods are always available to handle requests. NCC depends heavily on mechanisms built into Kubernetes that monitor and keep pods alive. There are additional ways NCC adds resiliency.
  • At the logical service level, redundancy mechanisms monitor logical groups of pods within a deployment. NCC validates that services are healthy enough to all work together and fully satisfy requests.
  • At the NCC system level, redundancy ensures that entire NCC systems can take over for each other. Whether a system is logically determined to be not capable of satisfying requests based on NCC monitoring, or if a system is physically unreachable, alternate systems are in place to take over traffic and ensure processing of external requests continues.

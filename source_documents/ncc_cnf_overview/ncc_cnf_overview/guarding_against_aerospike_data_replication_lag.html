Guarding against Aerospike data replication lag

NCC monitors to ensure data replication between subsystems does not fall too far behind. The Aerospike XDR component is responsible for transferring data, and it reports back on its current lag for both subsystems.

Within a pair of subsystems sharing data, NCC monitors for a remote subsystem that reports itself to be lagging higher than a configured tolerance.

If the remote subsystem reports the lagging to be higher than the tolerable and configured value, the local subsystem forces itself to go out of service. The reasoning is that if the remote subsystem is reporting a lag, some of the data on the local subsystem may be out of date. The current default lag threshold is set at 10 minutes.
